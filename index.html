Kangaroo

A few facts about Kangaroo's

The Kangaroo is a unique marsupial native to Australia. Known for its powerful hind legs, long tail, and distinctive hopping motion, the kangaroo is an iconic sumbol of the Australian wildlife. They have a muscular build, with males reaching heights up to 200 punds (90 kilograms).
Kangaroos are herbivores, primarily feeding on grasses and plants. They are also known for their pouch, where the female carries and nurtures their young, called joeys, until they are fully developed.
Kangaroos are well-adapted to their environment and play important ecological role in Australia's ecosystem.

About the Kangaroo

  • Species: Macropodidae
  • Uniquie Adaptations: Speed up to 35MPH
  • Size & Weight: Up to 6ft and around 200 pounds
  • Social Structure: Generally social animals
  • Nocturnal & Crepuscular: Active during early morning and late evening
